# Weekly status format guide

The body of every weekly status must follow this template **exactly**. Optimised for paste into Microsoft Teams or Slack — bullets and headings only, no tables.

## Canonical template

```markdown
---
artifact_type: weekly-status
engagement: <slug>
week: <YYYY-Www>
period_start: <YYYY-MM-DD>
period_end: <YYYY-MM-DD>
charter_revision: <N>
generated_by: pdf-weekly-status
generated_at: <iso-timestamp>
red_teamed: false
sources:
  - 00-constitution/CHARTER.md
  - 04-execution/RAID.md
  - decision-log.md
  - 04-execution/standups/<latest>
---

# Weekly status — <Engagement name> — Week of <Monday date>

**Headline:** <one sentence, ≤ 25 words. The thing that matters if they read nothing else.>

**Status:** **<GREEN | AMBER | RED>** — <one-line justification>

## What moved

- <bullet — specific and named; e.g. "Identity-service migration: 6 of 8 services cut over; rollback drill completed">
- <bullet>
- <bullet>

## What slipped

- <bullet — what slipped, by how much, why; e.g. "Reporting module: 3 days late. Cause: vendor API change.">
- <bullet>

(Omit this section if nothing slipped. Don't pad.)

## Decisions made

- DEC-NNN — <one-line decision> (<decided_by>)
- DEC-NNN — <one-line decision> (<decided_by>)

(Omit if no consequential decisions this week.)

## Risks and issues

- <R-NNN | I-NNN> <title> — <change this week>  (NEW | escalated | mitigated | closed)
- ...

(Risks and issues that did not move this week are not listed.)

## Next week — top 3

1. <priority>
2. <priority>
3. <priority>

## Asks

- From <name>: need <what>, by <when>.
- From <name>: need <what>, by <when>.

(Omit if no asks. Don't invent asks to fill space.)

---

_Generated by `pdf-weekly-status` against charter revision <N>. Reconciles to: `CHARTER.md`, `RAID.md`, `decision-log.md`._
```

## Why this shape

- **Headline first.** Many recipients won't read past the headline; that's a feature, not a bug.
- **No tables.** Tables paste badly into Teams and Slack; the markdown often becomes a wall of pipes. Bullets are universal.
- **GREEN/AMBER/RED with justification.** A colour without a one-liner is theatre.
- **What moved / what slipped — both visible.** The asymmetry of only reporting good news is the most common status-report failure.
- **Decisions in line.** A weekly status that omits decisions made forces the next reader to chase the decision log. Cite the IDs; the log lives in `decision-log.md`.
- **Asks at the bottom, fully-formed.** "From X, need Y, by Z." Vague asks ("we could use some help with…") never land.
- **Don't pad.** Omit empty sections. A four-bullet status is fine.

## Anti-patterns to refuse

- **Tables in the body.** Move to a separate appendix file if a table is essential; the body stays bullet-only.
- **Adjective inflation.** "Solid progress", "excellent collaboration", "great momentum" — strip on validate. Replace with specifics or delete.
- **Generic risks.** "Resource constraints", "scope creep", "external dependencies" without specifics are not RAID entries; they are filler.
- **Future-tense achievements.** "Next week we will deliver X" belongs in *Next week*, not in *What moved*.
- **More than three priorities in Next week.** Three is the limit. If the user offers five, ask them to pick three and label the rest "stretch".

## Length guideline

A well-formed weekly status is **150–400 words** in the body. Below 100 is usually under-cooked; above 600 is usually padded. The validation step does not enforce a word count, but the workflow should suggest tightening if outside this range.
