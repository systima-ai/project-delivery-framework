# Performance conversation prep workflow

Produces `_pdf-output/engagements/{active}/09-people/performance-conversations/<YYYY-MM-DD>-<person-slug>-<topic-slug>.md`. **Highly confidential** — content includes performance assessments and intended challenge.

**Model:** Radical Candor (Kim Scott). Care Personally + Challenge Directly. See `references/radical-candor-guide.md` for the 2×2 framework, the four quadrants, and the practices that move a conversation toward the Radical Candor quadrant.

Iris's principle 5 is the load-bearing rule: performance conversations are owned, not delegated. The prep does not write the conversation; it scaffolds the manager's thinking before they own the room.

## Preconditions

- Active engagement
- The individual exists in STAKEHOLDERS.md
- A specific topic to address (this is not a general 1:1; if it's general, route to `pdf-prep-1on1`)

## Intent: create

1. **Identify the person and topic.** One person, one specific topic per file.
2. **Locate this conversation in the Radical Candor 2×2.** Walk the user through:
   - *"How much do they know you care about them — based on your behaviour, not your intent?"* (Care axis)
   - *"How directly have you been challenging them on this topic, historically?"* (Challenge axis)
   - The workflow names the resulting quadrant (Radical Candor / Ruinous Empathy / Obnoxious Aggression / Manipulative Insincerity).
   - If anywhere other than Radical Candor: ask which axis needs to shift, and how.
3. **Walk the SBI structure** (situation-behaviour-impact) for the substance — even though the framework is Radical Candor, the *language* uses SBI:
   - **Situation:** specific time, specific context (not "in general", not "always")
   - **Behaviour:** what they did or didn't do, observable
   - **Impact:** what consequence this had — for them, for the team, for the engagement
4. **Identify the desired outcome.** *"In one sentence, what does success look like at the end of this conversation?"*
5. **Anticipate their response.** *"What's the most likely reaction? Defensive, agreeing, deflecting, surprised? What's your plan for each?"*
6. **Plan the delivery.** *"How will you open? Where will you sit? How long do you intend the conversation to be? Who else knows it's happening?"*

8. **Integrity check (light, not full red-team).**
   - *Is the SBI specific? "He's been disengaged" is not behaviour; "in the last three retros he hasn't spoken without being prompted" is.*
   - *Is the impact framed for them, not just for you? "I've found it frustrating" is partial; "the team has been operating without your input on X" is impact.*
   - *Have you assumed agreement? The reactions table reveals optimism bias.*
   - *Is the opener honest? "How's it going?" hides intent; "I want to talk about something I've been observing — is now a good time?" signals what's coming.*

9. **Cross-skill offers.**
   - If the conversation is about exit-intent → also recommend `pdf-attrition-risk` (re-score).
   - If the conversation may trigger HR involvement → flag in the file's "Who else knows".
   - If the topic affects an existing 1:1 cadence → recommend `pdf-prep-1on1` update to log that this conversation is happening.

10. **Write.**

## Intent: update (record what happened)

1. Identify by person + topic.
2. Populate the "After the conversation" section.
3. Update frontmatter; set a follow-up date.
4. If the conversation went badly — outcome diverged significantly from desired — the workflow asks: *"Schedule a retro on this prep? What would you do differently?"*

## Intent: validate

- [ ] Frontmatter complete; `confidential: true`; quadrants set
- [ ] SBI is specific (not "in general", "always", "never")
- [ ] Desired outcome is one sentence
- [ ] At least three anticipated reactions covered
- [ ] Delivery plan covers opener, setting, time, who-else-knows, follow-up
- [ ] "What you do NOT want to do" section is non-empty (the anti-patterns are personal; if you can't name them, the prep is shallow)

## Intent: dump-merge

Accept dumped material — frustration vent, draft of what you wanted to say, a draft email you decided not to send. The workflow extracts the substantive SBI and reshapes; explicitly strips emotional language during the reshape (without losing the substance).

## Anti-patterns to refuse

- **General "performance issues" not tied to specific Situation+Behaviour.** Refuse. Generality is the failure mode; the prep exists to force specificity.
- **A "conversation" that's really a verdict.** If the desired outcome is "tell them they're being managed out" and the anticipated reactions don't include any branching, this is a notification, not a conversation. The workflow flags this and asks whether HR is engaged.
- **No follow-up date.** Refuse. A conversation with no follow-up is unenforced.
- **Care axis high; Challenge axis high; quadrant still labelled "Ruinous Empathy".** Internal consistency check; the workflow ensures the quadrant label matches the axis assessment.

## Red-team posture

Off. The integrity check above is the equivalent — narrower, focused on the specific failure modes of performance prep (vagueness, optimism bias, hidden verdict, missing follow-up).

The only exception: if the user is going to share the prep with HR or with a coach for review, the workflow offers a lightweight "share-prep tone check" before the user exports it.
