# ROM estimate workflow

Produces `_pdf-output/engagements/{active}/01-shaping/rom-estimate.md`. Single file per engagement.

**The estimate is always a range, never a point.** The headline shape is a **cone of uncertainty** — explicit about how precise the number is given how mature the opportunity is. See `references/cone-of-uncertainty.md` for the canonical cone stages and what each one means.

Red-team is **default on** for this workflow because the number that comes out of it will anchor every subsequent commercial conversation.

## Preconditions

- `pdf-shape-opportunity` complete with a recommended option named
- `pdf-qualify-opportunity` complete (informs which cone stage applies)

## Intent: create

1. **Read shape.** Pull the recommended option's scope and indicative team shape.
2. **Determine cone stage.** Ask:
   - *"What's the maturity of this opportunity? (Feasibility / Concept / Scope-agreed / Design-complete / Build-complete.)"*
   - For shape-stage work, this is usually **Feasibility** (±50% to ±100%) or **Concept** (±33% to ±50%).
3. **Walk the costing.**
   - For each role in the recommended option's team shape: rate × FTE × duration.
   - Rates from engagement `.pdf-config.toml` if present; otherwise elicit.
   - Add pass-through cost ranges (vendor, infrastructure, travel).
   - Add contingency *transparently* — named, sized, and visible in the output (not buried).
4. **Apply the cone.** The point computation (low + middle + high) is multiplied through the cone-stage range.
5. **Produce both the cone view and the operating range.**
6. **Compose:**

```markdown
---
artifact_type: rom-estimate
engagement: <slug>
date: <YYYY-MM-DD>
cone_stage: <feasibility | concept | scope-agreed | design-complete | build-complete>
cone_range_low_pct: <-50>
cone_range_high_pct: <+100>
point_estimate: <£N>
range_low: <£N>
range_high: <£N>
currency: GBP
linked_shape: 01-shaping/opportunity-shape.md
linked_option: <Small | Medium | Large>
generated_by: pdf-estimate-rom
red_teamed: false
---
```

7. **Run simulated red-team inline:**
   - *Which assumption hides a big cost we haven't sized?*
   - *Is contingency real or theatre? At feasibility stage, < 20% contingency is suspicious.*
   - *Are we anchoring on the client's reference numbers rather than our build-up?*
   - *Is the operating range narrow enough that we'll squirm if asked to deliver at the low end?*
   - *Is there a hidden assumption about access — to data, to people, to systems — that we haven't surfaced?*
8. **Write the file.** Frontmatter `red_teamed` set per red-team result.
9. **Hand off:** `pdf-draft-sow` (the SoW operationalises this number).

## Intent: update

1. Common updates: cone stage advances (shape firms up); rates change; assumptions move; pass-throughs concrete.
2. The cone range narrows as the stage advances; the workflow enforces this — you cannot keep a wide cone with no new uncertainty.
3. Append `## Revisions` section with what changed.

## Intent: validate

- [ ] Cone stage is named
- [ ] Cone range matches the stage (per `references/cone-of-uncertainty.md`)
- [ ] Operating range is wider than the point estimate by at least the cone factors
- [ ] Contingency is named and sized; suspicious if < 20% at feasibility
- [ ] At least 3 assumptions surfaced with sensitivity sizing
- [ ] Sensitivity section names the highest-impact assumption
- [ ] Frontmatter `red_teamed: true`

## Intent: dump-merge

Accept dumped material (client budget hint, prior similar engagement actuals, vendor quote). Use to inform the build-up; flag where dumped material is being absorbed and surface anchoring risk (client budget hints anchor estimators; the workflow asks whether the build-up matches or has been bent).

## Anti-patterns to refuse

- **Single-point estimate.** Refuse. The whole point of ROM is the range.
- **Contingency embedded in line items.** Refuse. Contingency is surfaced as its own line.
- **Cone narrower than the stage warrants.** Workflow flags — narrowing the cone before the stage matures is dishonest.
- **Anchoring on client numbers.** If the user mentions "they said budget is around £X", the workflow asks: does the build-up confirm this, or are we adjusting to fit?

## Red-team posture

**Default ON.** Simulated red-team inline until `pdf-red-team` exists.
