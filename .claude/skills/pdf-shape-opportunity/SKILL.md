# Opportunity shape workflow

Produces `_pdf-output/engagements/{active}/01-shaping/opportunity-shape.md`. Single file per engagement.

The output is a **structured hypothesis** about what the engagement is, what it isn't, and what assumptions it rests on. The three-sizing-options structure is the framework's bet against single-point opportunity definition: most opportunities can be delivered at three scales, and naming all three forces the buyer (and you) to decide which scale you're talking about.

## Preconditions

- `pdf-qualify-opportunity` complete with recommendation `GO` or `GO-with-conditions`

## Intent: create

1. **Read the qualification memo.** Pull MEDDIC findings; specifically Metrics (what they want improved), Identify Pain (why now), Decision Criteria (what they'll evaluate against).
2. **Walk the shape elicitation.**

   ### Hypothesis
   *"In two sentences, what is this engagement actually about — what does it deliver, and what's the underlying job-to-be-done? Not the client's exact words; your shaped hypothesis."*

   ### Three sizing options

   For each of Small / Medium / Large:
   - Scope (what's in)
   - What's deliberately not in
   - Indicative duration (in weeks or months; coarse)
   - Indicative team shape (FTE-months by role; coarse)
   - Best-fit client situation (when is this the right size?)

   The three options must be genuinely different — not "small but slow", "medium and normal", "large but expensive". Different in **scope shape**, not just scale.

   ### Assumptions

   List the assumptions the shape rests on. Each: what's assumed, why, what invalidates it.

   ### Risks (shape-stage)

   Top 5 risks specific to this opportunity at this stage. Include both delivery risks (we estimated wrong, scope is hidden) and commercial risks (decision drifts, budget shifts).

   ### Recommended option

   Which of the three sizing options you recommend; one paragraph of reasoning; acknowledges trade-offs.

4. **Cross-skill side effects.**
   - Always → offer `pdf-estimate-rom` next.
   - The recommended option becomes the input to ROM (sizing is the headline driver).
   - Material assumptions → can be appended to `RAID.md` as Assumptions once the engagement is initialised post-signature (via Marcus's update intent).

5. **Write.**

## Intent: update

1. Common updates: a sizing option becomes definitive (no longer three; the buyer picked); assumptions move from "assumed" to "confirmed" or "invalidated"; new risks surface.
2. The recommended_option in frontmatter may change.

## Intent: validate

- [ ] Frontmatter complete; `qualification_outcome` is GO or GO-with-conditions
- [ ] Three sizing options present, each with all five fields populated
- [ ] Options are genuinely different in scope shape, not just scale (workflow flags this if Small / Medium / Large look like the same engagement scaled up)
- [ ] At least 3 assumptions with all three columns filled
- [ ] Top-5 risks: exactly 5 (rare to have fewer at this stage; if so, flag as suspicious confidence)
- [ ] Recommended option named with reasoning

## Intent: dump-merge

Accept dumped material (RFP, client conversation transcript, internal scoping notes). Extract candidate scope items, assumptions, risks. Structure into the three-option model; if material naturally suggests only one scale, the workflow asks for two alternatives even so (forces option-thinking).

## Anti-patterns to refuse

- **One sizing option dressed as three.** Validation checks that the three options differ in scope shape, not just headcount or duration.
- **No "best-fit when" per option.** Refuse; the option must be situated, not just sized.
- **Assumptions without "what invalidates it".** Refuse; an unfalsifiable assumption is a wish.
- **Recommended option with no acknowledged trade-off.** Refuse; the recommendation must name what's being given up.

## Red-team posture

Off. Internal hypothesis. The downstream ROM and SoW red-team.
